What Happened to the Beach? : Declan McKenna

Pop Rock: 8/10

The main reason I chose Declan's latest album as my first ever review is because I am going to see him in concert in 3 weeks. This is his third major-label album, and it demonstrates a great advance in his creativity abilities. The songs experiment with psychedelic sounds and most consist of an electric feel. Even though I saw mixed reviews on this album, I thought it was a great project (although it did take a few listens to really appreciate each song). Each track was unique through its own experimental sounds and instruments; but a full listen felt very put together, like each song belongs. This is the primary factor that went into rating this record so high; each song caught my attention through their individuality. Not only did I appreciate the musicality of this album but also the lyrics. I tend to be more drawn to a songs’ beat rather than lyrics, but I did like how each song had its own story and meaning. This aided in creating a whimsical and cosmic feel, rather than each song connecting. After a few listens, I’d say my top 3 songs are Nothing Works, Mezzanine, and I Write The News. These songs stuck with me mainly due to their instrumentation. But after understanding the meanings of the other two, I enjoy them even more. Nothing Works roots from Declan’s record label telling him to go back to his earlier style of music. Declan himself stated that this track “came out of the frustration of feeling boxed in and tied to expectation.” I find the fact that he stuck to his true self inspiring since there are many artists who would not have done the same. If you are familiar with other work by Declan Mckenna, you know that in his writing he explores his political thoughts. In this media-saturated era, it has become extremely difficult for the facts to be seen by everyone; each side believes that is the only truth, and that is what I Write The News is communicating.
